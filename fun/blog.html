Meaning of Life

Life is not worth living if you have everything you need. The easier the struggles come to you, the faster you will find meaning. However, not having enough resources to realize your potential might be the most devastating thing one could suffer from.

A delicate balance must be striken between hardship and potential. An environment too harsh and life will wither and die. An environment too accomodating and life will thrive, grow without regulation and out of control.

Humans have something extra special — the ability to form and transform thoughts between the abstract and the concrete. We call it knowledge. But giving knowledge to a people who haven’t lived enough to understand it is no different from giving them poison.
